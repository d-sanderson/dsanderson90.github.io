Weekly Reflections

Week 1 Reflection:

Holy ****, What an exciting first week! It's been a whirlwind but it feels amazing to be here. We covered quite a bit this week - ranging from basic HTML, git repos, server deployment, to data design and more. I now feel comfortable throwing up basic HTML websites to the server and laughing at how they looks like they are from the 90's. I can't wait to get better at CSS and Javascript so I can bring my sites into the modern age. On Wednesday I went to the React505 meetup and was blown away by the technology. It made me even more exited to learn Javascript! I love how responsive React is and how if you break something, it is immediately reflected on the browser without a refresh. Also learning about the virtual DOM was super fascinating. This week has been both challenging and inspiring. I know I have a lot to learn and that the weeks ahead will be arduous but my hopes balance out the stress and trepidations. I can't wait to learn more!

Week 2 Reflection:

Another week down! This week was filled with lots of ups and downs. Programming is a puzzle and the process is just as enjoyable as the product. Each day I am immersed in a language that I don't understand confronted by concepts that are equally perplexing. It's hard that for sure but damn if it isn't rewarding. I have found that I enjoy making something new and the anticipation I experience when I test it. Will it work or will it throw me a bunch of errors? Either way I will persist. I relish in the debugging as much I like building something new. What piece isn't working? Is there a flaw in my logic? What do I need to know to make this work? Even if I can get one piece to work I'll be one step closer to solving the larger problem. Some of the concepts we learned this week included Object Orient programming using PHP and Javascript. The data design project kicked my ass a bit but things are starting to fall into place. I am beginning to understand Clasess and how constructors make it easier to assign values(states) to our objects. I also had an aha moment when I read that Classes live in a lib file and that the can be exported and imported into the file in which we work with them. It was also really cool to see that a long snipper of code can be packaged into one variable and then executed. I look forward to what I will learn next week.

Passion Project

I started teaching myself how to play Piano 4 years ago. I've always been a musical person but told myself that it was too late in my life to learn an instrument. When my desire to create finally overcame my doubts, I got on craigslist and found myself a basic keyboard and practicing for an hour each day. I really wanted to be able to play my favorites songs and some idical music that I had heard growing up with my Dad. I made a lot of progress in the first year and often would go over my scheduled hour of practice just because I was enjoying learning. Eventually I bought a sweet Yamaha Electronic Keyboard with weighted keys. I love playing music because it brings me into a focused state where all of my worries and doubts sort of fade to the background. I love being so absorbed in a task that I cannot think of the past or future. It is interesting because in a flow state your mind is both relaxed and engaged in the task at hand. I continue to pratice piano and love learning new songs.
